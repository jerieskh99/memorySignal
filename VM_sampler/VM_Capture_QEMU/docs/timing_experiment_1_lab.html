In one breathThe pause-the-video analogy

Imagine watching a film while someone keeps hitting pause every few seconds to photograph the screen. The film's running time is unchanged вЂ” characters age at the same rate, scenes last the same number of minutes. But your evening gets very long because every pause adds real seconds to the wall clock without advancing the story.

The guest VM is the film. The host orchestrator is the photographer. pmemsave is the photograph. Every snapshot pauses the film for ~1вЂ“2 host-seconds, takes the photo, then unpauses. The film's timeline (Axis A) is short and contiguous from the guest's perspective. The photographer's evening (Axis B) is long and full of waiting.

Every later confusion about timing reduces to "which clock are we reading right now". The analyzer must read the film's clock (Axis A) when assigning periods to spectral peaks; the operator reads the wall clock (Axis B) when estimating how long an experiment will take.

Why it matters В· technicalWhich axis controls which knob

simple terms

Axis A is what your analysis measures. Axis B is what your patience measures. Get them mixed up and a 0.1 Hz workload rhythm looks like a 0.007 Hz rhythm (or vice versa).

spectral

For a cepstral peak at frame index k, the period in physical time is k Г— О”t. The correct О”t is Axis A (= intervalMsec + ~25 ms). Using Axis B (the per-snapshot wall-clock cost) inflates every period by ~15Г— on this host.

Geometry В· caseWhy the fill width is "cycles per window"

The fill width on each bar is literally the ratio W / T capped at some upper bound for display. A bar at "full width" means the window contains the workload's rhythm many times over вЂ” easy to detect, easy to characterise. A bar at "tiny sliver" means the workload's rhythm needs many windows just to fit one period вЂ” impossible to detect inside a single window.

That makes the bars an honest discriminability proxy. Two workloads whose bars are both full-width but at different green shades still separate; two workloads with both bars at slivers are doomed regardless of model choice.

To map a specific scenario: at intervalMsec=100 the window is 16 s. io_seq_fsync's rhythm is 1 s в†’ bar shows W/T = 16 cycles в†’ full green. sandbox_ransom_batched's rhythm is 60 s в†’ bar shows W/T в‰€ 0.27 cycles в†’ tiny red sliver. Even though the workloads are structurally very different, their bars converge to the same visual signal at the broken configuration: "incomplete information, classifier can't tell".

Build the intuitionWhat "discriminability" actually means

Before we can say the four mechanisms multiply, we need a clear picture of what they multiply against. The thing being eroded is called discriminability: how much numerical distance separates the feature vectors of two distinct workloads.

Imagine each workload produces a point cloud in feature space вЂ” many windows per recording, each contributing one point. Two well-separated workloads produce two cleanly-distinct clouds with a wide gap between them. The classifier draws a boundary in that gap. Two confused workloads produce overlapping clouds; no boundary can cleanly separate them.

Discriminability geometry
CLEAN CASE DEGRADED CASE CONFUSED CASE в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€ В·В·В·В·В·В·В·В· В·В·В·В·В·В·В·В·В·В· в–€в–€в–€Aв–€в–€в–€ в–“в–“в–“в–“в–“в–“в–“в–“ в–‘в–‘В·AВ·в–‘в–‘в–‘ в–“в–‘в–‘в–‘в–‘в–‘в–“ В·AВ·в–“В·BВ·В·В·В·В· в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€в–€ в–“в–“Bв–“в–“в–“в–“ В·В·в–‘в–‘в–‘в–‘В· в–“в–“в–“Bв–‘в–“в–‘ В·В·в–‘в–‘в–‘В·в–‘в–‘в–‘в–‘в–‘ в–“в–“в–“в–“в–“в–“ в–‘в–‘в–‘В·в–‘ В·в–‘в–‘в–‘в–“в–“в–“ classifier guesses clear gap margin narrows 50 % of the time accuracy ~100 % accuracy ~80 % accuracy ~50 %

Each mechanism we're about to describe takes a knife to the gap. Mechanism (i) collapses both clouds toward the middle. (ii) shifts both clouds in the same wrong direction. (iii) blurs each cloud outward. (iv) inflates the noise floor under both. None of these alone wipe out separation entirely. Together they squeeze the gap until the classifier can't find a boundary.

The point of saying "multiply" is that we cannot fix three of them and expect 75 % recovery. Each surviving mechanism keeps a multiplicative chunk of the damage. The chain has to be unbroken end to end.

Plain languageThe four mechanisms don't add вЂ” they multiply

With the gap-geometry picture in mind: imagine each mechanism survives some fraction f of the gap that existed before it acted. A perfect pipeline preserves f = 1.0. A mechanism that halves the discriminability has f = 0.5. If you stack four mechanisms with survival fractions fi, fii, fiii, fiv, the gap that reaches the classifier is the product fi В· fii В· fiii В· fiv.

Numerical example: a workload pair that loses 60 % of its margin to mechanism i (so fi = 0.4), another 40 % of what's left to ii (fii = 0.6), and another 30 % to iii (fiii = 0.7) keeps 0.4 Г— 0.6 Г— 0.7 = 0.168 в‰€ 17 % of its original discriminability. By the time the classifier sees the data, two genuinely distinct workloads look ~83 % identical.

That is the difference between a classifier you can interpret and a confusion matrix you can't trust. Each individual mechanism looks survivable on its own вЂ” fix the worst one and you'd expect a clean dataset. The product math says otherwise: even one surviving mechanism with f = 0.3 caps your recoverable signal at 30 %. Fixing only some of them leaves a low ceiling.

Those would push deeper into the clean band (5.3 and 10.6 periods/window respectively) but at a cost: a small workload-internal sub-rhythm at, say, 1 s would now be near the Nyquist edge at intervalMsec = 500 ms (2В·О”t = 1.05 s = T) and outright aliased at 1 s. 250 ms is the smallest intervalMsec that pushes A's main rhythm clear of the marginal band while still keeping ~0.5 s sub-rhythms safely above Nyquist. Goldilocks choice for this pair.

For a workload pair with much slower rhythms (say sandbox_ransom_batched's 60 s phases) we'd push further to 1 s. Plan 02 (per-family intervalMsec profile) is exactly this calculation done per workload family.

Run 1: confusion в‰€ 50 % (random).

Round 2 at intervalMsec=250 ms: separation recovered; classifier should converge.

Memorable imageA song identifier on broken hardware

Imagine trying to identify which song is playing from 6-millisecond audio clips taken every fraction of a second, using a stopwatch that drifts unpredictably. Each clip is too short to contain even one phrase of the lyrics. The stopwatch drift means consecutive clips don't sit at known time offsets. Every song you sample sounds like white noise.

That is what the analyzer faced in Run 1. Mechanism i made the clips too short; ii put each clip's timestamp on the wrong axis; iii made the stopwatch unreliable; iv made every song sound like it had the same background hiss.

The Round 2 fix is "use a real 16-second sample at the correct guest-time rate, on a stopwatch that runs steady". Suddenly the same songs are easy to tell apart.

Round 2 nuanceWhy mechanism vi only appeared after the others were fixed

In Run 1, every measurement was so broken that mechanism vi was invisible вЂ” pmemsave drift from 0.77 s to 4.7 s is irrelevant when guest_dt itself is mis-calibrated by 15Г—. Only after fixing the bc bug, killing the consumer, and adding the start-of-pass purge could we see the producer's accumulation problem clearly: a clean drift profile against a baseline that was actually stable.

The general pattern: each layer of bug masks the next. Plan 1 (producer self-clean) is expected to be the last structural issue, because the layers below it are now solid. If after Plan 1 the analyzer still struggles, the next suspect is window/hop choice, not the capture pipeline.

Plain languageWhat each test actually does

The chain has three small tests (2a / 2b / 2c). Each isolates one specific question. None of them touches data, and none of them touches the existing capture pipeline when it is not running. They are diagnostic probes, not part of the capture chain.

Test 2a В· the "kill the noisy roommate" test

Runs the producer twice for 60 s each. First with whatever the host state happens to be ("consumer on"). Second after pkill'ing the consumer ("consumer off"). Compares per-snapshot timing between the two passes. The question it answers: how much of the host-side variability is the consumer's fault?

technical

A/B comparison of host snapshot cycle distribution under two states: (1) consumer subprocess running and consuming queue, (2) consumer killed + queue drained. Producer-only timing in both. Reports paired means / medians / std / pause-fraction. Mechanism iii diagnostic.

Test 2b В· the "how slow can I sample" test

Runs the producer four times in a row, once for each of intervalMsec в€€ {100, 250, 500, 1000} ms. Each cell is 60 s of host wall-clock. The question it answers: how much does the VM spend paused as we slow down sampling вЂ” and where's the sweet spot?

technical

One-factor parametric sweep over intervalMsec, RAM held constant. Each cell measures the pause fraction (ОЈ paused / ОЈ wall-clock). Output is a monotonic curve; the operator picks an intervalMsec per workload family using this curve plus that family's rhythm-of-interest. Mechanism iv diagnostic.

Test 2c В· the "do we still need this brake?" test

The producer has a hard-coded sleep 0.5 after every pmemsave вЂ” a safety brake from early development. 2c runs the producer twice (with the brake on, with the brake off) and checks if the dumps are still readable in both. The question it answers: is the 0.5 s safety brake actually doing anything, or is it just slowing us down?

technical

Toggles TIMING_NO_FLUSH env var that gates the post-pmemsave sleep 0.5 in the producer. Compares host_dt distribution and dump integrity (file size + 3-point readability probe) between flush_on and flush_off. Reports a KEEP / REMOVE / NEUTRAL verdict. Cheap throughput win or "keep it for safety" decision.

Plain languageWhat each plan actually changes in the pipeline

Four small code changes. None of them adds a feature; all of them close a previously-identified gap. None touches the analyzer; all four are in the capture / orchestrator side.

Plan 1 В· "let the producer take out its own trash"

When the consumer is killed for timing isolation, no one is left to delete old dump files. Plan 1 adds an opt-in toggle: when TIMING_SELF_CLEAN=1 is set, the producer deletes the previous dump as soon as it has written the next one. Like the consumer's rolling-delete, but cooperative.

technical

Patch to capture_producer_qemu_pmemsave.sh: when env var TIMING_SELF_CLEAN is set, replace the queue-enqueue branch with sudo rm -f "$prevImage". Steady-state on-disk dump count stays at 2 (prev + curr). Eliminates mechanism vi (in-pass pmemsave drift). ~6 lines bash.

Plan 1b В· "tell the orchestrators to use it"

The producer change in Plan 1 is opt-in. Plan 1b makes 2a / 2b / 2c each set the env var when they launch the producer, so the new behaviour is automatic during timing experiments without affecting normal captures.

technical

env["TIMING_SELF_CLEAN"] = "1" in each orchestrator's producer-launch subprocess.Popen environment. Three files Г— ~3 lines.

Plan 3 В· "compare the clean part, not the drifty part"

2c's verdict was wrong because it averaged across 21 snapshots вЂ” including the last 10 which had drifted. Plan 3 makes the verdict use only the first 10 snapshots, where pmemsave is stable. Keeps both numbers in the JSON for transparency.

technical

Add stable_mean(records, key, n=10) helper to run_exp2c_flush_sensitivity.py. Recommendation logic uses mean_first_10 instead of mean_all. Both values reported in JSON. ~20 lines Python. Fixes mechanism vii.

Plan 4 В· "stop crying wolf on disk space"

The disk-free pre-check assumes every dump stays on disk for the whole run. After Plan 1 the producer cleans as it goes, so peak disk use is two dumps вЂ” tiny. Plan 4 makes the warning aware of that.

technical

Add peak_concurrent_dumps parameter to disk_free_check. Orchestrators pass 2 when self-clean is on, the snapshot count when it's off. Stops a false-positive WARNING that was misleading and made operators question valid runs. ~10 lines Python.

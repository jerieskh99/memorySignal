Research Log

A compressed “month” of analysis that turned the Phase-2 v3 results into a paper portfolio · run under the project manager

This log records how the portfolio was actually built: a data audit that re-grounded every number, a debate about how many papers the results support, an independent review round that overturned the headline result, and a revision pass that folded the critiques back in. The four phases are framed as four weeks for readability; the exchanges are reconstructions of the substantive arguments, and every number cited is from the on-disk artifacts.

Week 1 — kickoff and data audit

Goal

Before anyone writes a sentence, re-ground every headline number in the on-disk v3 artifacts. The PM's rule: no figure enters a draft unless Eng can point to the file it came from.

What the audit found
  • Scale. _v3_combined holds 132 cells: 11 workloads × 3 durations (120 / 300 / 600 s) × 4 replicates, all at a single requested 500 ms interval.
  • The interval knob is a fiction. Measured cadence is 15–21 s per snapshot, so a 120 s cell yields only 7 snapshots, 300 s yields 20, 600 s yields 28. Estimated guest-pause fraction 0.96–0.97; pmemsave itself ≈ 0.75 s. The cadence is set by the snapshot primitive, not the 500 ms request.
  • Tuning (plan03/recommendation.json). Ten of eleven workloads select window/hop (8,4); the bimodal hash-table selects (32,8). Five workloads clear every gate; the phasic family passes the defining-metric gate (cepstral SNR 4.78–6.35 dB) but fails spectral coverage (≈0.20 « 2.0).
  • Segmentation (plan04/). 10 of 11 workloads accepted (120 of 132 cells); one steady workload (mem_pagefault_density_v2) fails; phasic cells validated by plausibility, not marker-F1.
  • A provenance mismatch. The storage headline (4.7 MiB trajectory vs ~5 TiB per-page projection) is quoted against a 90-cell campaign, while the dataset is 132 cells. The PM flags this immediately.
Exchange
PM: The 500 ms interval is in every old slide. Is it true in the data?
Eng: No. The timings say 15–21 seconds a snapshot. The guest is paused 96–97% of the time. The interval never gets a chance to matter.
LR: Then that is not a caveat, that is a finding. The cost model, not the interval, sets temporal resolution. Write it down as finding number one.
PM: And the million-fold storage number — is the five terabytes measured?
Eng: It is a projection. We never retained the full per-page campaign; we could not, it would not fit. And the 4.7 MiB is the 90-cell figure.
PM: Then we label it a projection and reconcile the campaign size before anyone prints “106×.”
Decisions
  • Adopt the four honest findings as first-class results, starting with the cadence floor.
  • Keep both the 90-cell and 132-cell framings but label provenance everywhere; reconcile to one campaign size before submission.

Week 2 — the paper-conception debate

Goal

Decide what the results are, as publications. One mega-paper, or several focused ones? The LR argues the results split cleanly into four; the reviewers push back on the venue map.

The four candidate results
  • A — capture. The APF signal and its streaming delete-as-you-go pipeline, plus the measured cost floor.
  • B — tuning. The acceptance-gated grid search that certifies “signal present” vs. “adequately sampled.”
  • C — classification. What workload identity survives the coarse scalar.
  • D — segmentation. Family-aware CUSUM that knows when to stay silent.
Exchange
LR: Four results, four papers. Each has its own method and its own honest negative.
Reviewer A: Four results, yes. Four venues, no. The capture method and the tuning protocol are one systems story — the cost floor in A is the reason B fixes a single interval. Split them and each half looks thin.
Reviewer B: Same on the other side. The classifier and the segmenter are one workload-characterization story; the segmenter is the “what next” of the classifier. And do not even whisper “security” yet — there is no threat model in this dataset.
LR: Then we keep four skeletons as the analytical decomposition and write toward two submissions. The skeletons isolate the results; the venues bundle them.
PM: Agreed. Author all four; recommend two venues; leave the final mapping to where the work lands.
Decision
  • Produce four skeletons (A, B, C, D), each with introduction / contributions / results as bullets.
  • Working venue plan: A+B → one systems paper, C+D → one characterization paper.
  • No security framing until a threat model, base rate, and benign-phasic control exist.

Week 3 — the independent review round

Goal

The two reviewers read the draft skeletons and the first-draft essay independently. The PM expects polish notes. Instead, one critique overturns the portfolio's headline number. Full memos: reviewer_memo.html.

The bombshell blocking · B-1

The first-draft Paper C led with “100% phasic-vs-steady accuracy, a confusion matrix of [[54,0],[0,64]] without a single error.” Reviewer B refused it on sight.

Reviewer B: One hundred percent, from one number per snapshot? That is not a result, that is a leak. Something you feed the classifier already knows the family. Ablate every feature the analyzer computes per family and re-run it. I will wait.
Eng: (overnight) Three features are family-conditional. cv_workingset is only computed for steady cells, f1_phase only for phasic — complementary missingness that is the label. And coverage_ratio is a per-family constant: 0.133 for steady, 0.200 for phasic.
Eng: coverage_ratio alone scores 1.000. It reproduces the entire headline by itself. Drop the three family-conditional features and accuracy falls to 0.644, against a 0.542 majority baseline.
LR: So the model was rediscovering our labelling rule, not memory behavior. The hundred percent is feature construction.
Reviewer B: Correct. And your stated mechanism is wrong too — you claimed phasic SNR is high and steady SNR is low. Steady SNR runs 2.65 to 7.99 dB; your steady hash-table workload has the highest SNR in the set. SNR alone classifies at 0.508. Chance.
PM: Then the headline is retracted, today. The question is what the honest paper is.
The systems critique major · A-1/A-2
Reviewer A: Paper A is your strongest standalone, but stop leading with “106×.” The numerator is a projection and the cells do not match. And the cadence floor — your best finding — is asserted. Decompose the 15–21 seconds: suspend, pmemsave, resume, helper read-back. Show me where the time goes.
PM: Both accepted. Honesty note into Paper A, budget decomposition onto the figure list, projection labelled.
What the room agreed
The negatives are the contribution. By the end of the round the team agreed that the four honest findings — cadence floor, coverage limit, feature-construction leakage, and marker non-observability — are more publishable, and more useful to the next capture, than the positive numbers beside them. Hiding any of them would weaken every claim.

Week 4 — revision and consolidation

Goal

Fold every accepted critique into the files and freeze the portfolio.

Changes made
  • Paper C recast. Retitled to a leakage-controlled study, retargeted to ML-for-systems / measurement, security claim removed. New sections: the leakage audit (full / coverage-alone / agnostic / baseline table), the retraction of the SNR mechanism, a Clopper–Pearson bound (0.969) on the face-value 100%, and a security-future-work list (threat model, real-malware corpus, benign-phasic control, open-set, ROC at base rate).
  • Ablation committed as code. paperC_family_classification/leakage_ablation.py and its .json output make the finding reproducible (scikit-learn 1.7.2).
  • Synthesis essay rewritten. It now leads with the trade — the 100% is an artifact, the leakage-free family signal is weak (0.644 vs 0.542) — and carries four honest findings, not three.
  • Paper A honesty notes. Storage projection labelled, campaign-size reconciliation flagged, cadence-budget decomposition added to the figure list, offline-capture framing strengthened.
  • Paper B confirmed. The gate framework stands; its value is exactly the G3-vs-G2 split (rhythm present, coverage insufficient). No claim was overstated, so no retraction — a validated result kept as-is.
  • Paper D confirmed. Leads with the observability finding (zero marker-rich cells, compounded by capture-side wall-clock skew); strong “we segment correctly” claim explicitly deferred.
Final shape

Four skeletons, one synthesis essay, a shared (placeholder) bibliography, and this log plus the reviewer memo. Working venue plan: two submissions. The portfolio index summarizes the numbers; the essay is the umbrella narrative; the skeletons are the publishable decompositions.

Open items — instructions to the next capture

  • Reconcile campaign size. Re-quote storage against the 132-cell campaign; report a measured numerator or label the per-page figure a projection. (A-1)
  • Decompose the cadence budget. Measure suspend / pmemsave / resume / helper read-back per snapshot. (A-2)
  • Lift coverage past G2. The phasic rhythm is real (SNR > 4.5 dB) but under-sampled (coverage ≈ 0.20). Buy more periods — faster snapshots or longer cells — not a different window. (Plan 05: producer throughput.)
  • Make markers survive into the signal. Phase labels must manifest as APF mean shifts; fix the capture-side wall-clock skew (guest time sync) so marker-aligned F1 becomes measurable. (B-6 / D)
  • Strengthen Paper C's evaluation. Switch to leave-one-workload-out CV with seeded repeats and reported confidence intervals. (B-4)
  • Fix the steady outlier. mem_pagefault_density_v2 injects spurious boundaries and fails the steady gate (stationarity 0.67, median spurious 1.0, P0=0.33).
  • Wire the G4 regression guard. Currently a placeholder that always passes.
  • Verify the bibliography. Most of refs.bib is TODO placeholders.

Executive summary

The thesis observes a virtual machine through a single scalar per snapshot — the Active Page Fraction (APF), the fraction of 4 KiB guest pages that change between consecutive full-memory dumps. The Phase-2 v3 campaign captured 132 cells across 11 workloads. The team's central thesis, which every paper inherits, is a single trade:

Collapsing a per-page 2-D change signal to one scalar destroys workload instance identity and leaves at most a weak, leakage-controlled hint of workload family (bursty vs. steady): 0.644 accuracy vs. a 0.542 baseline once family-conditional features are removed — a gap not significant at the replicate-group level. The apparent perfect separation is feature construction, not behavior.

The four honest findings

The team agreed these negative results are more publishable than the positive numbers beside them, and that hiding them would weaken every claim.

1. Snapshot-dominated cadence. The effective cadence (median ~10 s/snap, variable 2–24 s) is set by pmemsave (median 7 s, ~81% of the cycle), not the 500 ms knob. Because the cycle dwarfs the interval, sampling-interval tuning is inert — "how cheap is a snapshot" replaces "how often to sample."
2. Coverage limit. Phasic rhythm is detectable (cepstral SNR > 4.5 dB) but under-sampled (coverage ~0.20 « 2.0). The signature exists; too few periods fit in a cell. The fix is more periods, not a different window.
3. Feature-construction leakage. The "100%" phasic-vs-steady separation is an artifact: coverage_ratio is a deterministic per-family constant (0.1333 steady / 0.200 phasic, zero within-family variance), so it reproduces the label by construction. The leakage-free signal is weak and not significant at the replicate-group level — 0.644 vs. a 0.542 baseline (~33 independent groups). Per-class "defining metrics" must never feed a classifier meant to prove discrimination.
4. Marker non-observability. Workload phase markers describe internal events that never surface as APF mean shifts at this cadence, so marker-aligned segmentation F1 is structurally unavailable. Ground-truth phase labels must be designed to survive into the recorded signal.

Team & process

The portfolio was produced under a project manager by a lead researcher, two IEEE reviewers, and the engineering group, across a compressed analysis cycle. The workflow: a data audit that re-grounded every headline number in the on-disk artifacts; a paper-conception debate; independent reviewer critiques; and a revision pass that folded the critiques back into the skeletons. The narrative and the critiques are recorded in the research log and the reviewer memo.

Data provenance: _v3_combined — 132 cells, 11 workloads, durations {120, 300, 600}s, 4 replicates, single 500 ms requested interval. Classification uses the 118 cells usable at (W,H)=(8,4); segmentation covers all 132. Storage magnitudes are quoted against the 90-cell framing where noted.

Reviewer Memo — Round 2

A data-provenance audit: every quantitative claim in the frozen portfolio re-derived from the on-disk capture artifacts · conducted read-only over _v3_combined

How to read this

Round 1 (reviewer_memo.html) was a reasoning review: two reviewers read the draft and caught a leakage artifact that demolished the 100% classification headline. Round 2 is a provenance review of a different kind. It does not argue about method; it re-computes every number the portfolio quotes directly from the per-cell capture JSON and checks whether the figure printed in the paper is the figure on disk.

It mostly is not. The capture-cost section of Paper A, the cadence paragraph of the essay, and the capture-substrate table of the index all quoted the minimum of a wide, skewed distribution as if it were the typical value, and described one architectural feature (delete-as-you-go) that the v3 run did not actually exercise. The qualitative thesis survives intact — in one place it is now stronger — but the specific numbers were wrong and have been corrected throughout. This memo records each correction against the measurement that forced it.

Severity legend: blocking a claimed contribution or result was not supported by the run · major a printed number was materially wrong · minor rounding / tightening.

Provenance of this round. The audit was run with read-only tooling (file reads and jq aggregations) scoped to the papers directory and _v3_combined. No capture was re-run, no workload was launched, nothing was written outside this docs/papers tree. Every number below is reproducible from _v3_combined/cells/*.json and _v3_combined/plan03/sweep.csv.

What round 2 checked

The v3 capture wrote one JSON record per cell with a producer_stats block (per-snapshot timing) and a run_meta block (duration, workload, keep_dumps, exit status). Round 2 aggregated those blocks across the 128 cells with exit_status == "ok" (of 136 records / 132 nominal cells) and compared the resulting distributions to the figures printed in the portfolio. The classification numbers were re-checked against plan03/sweep.csv (the analyzer's per-cell feature table) and the leakage ablation JSON.

The single recurring error: the portfolio reported per-duration minima — the fastest, shortest cells — as representative. The capture cost is heavy-tailed and snapshot-dominated, so the minimum and the median differ by an order of magnitude.

Findings & actions

The audit was conducted by two reviewers reprising their round-1 lenses, now reading the data rather than the prose.

A
Reviewer A — systems / measurement. Re-derived the capture-cost distribution from producer_stats.
B
Reviewer B — ML / statistics. Re-checked the residual-signal significance and the gate degrees-of-freedom.
R-1  major  The capture-cost numbers are the distribution's minimum, quoted as if typical. Every headline capture figure — pmemsave ≈ 0.75 s, "7/20/28" snapshots, a "15–21 s floor", "7–28-point" trajectories, pause "0.96–0.97" — is at or near the fast/short end of a wide, right-skewed distribution. The medians are roughly an order of magnitude away: pmemsave median 7.06 s, snapshots 14/26/53, cycle median 9.75 s (range 2–24), trajectory median 30 (range 4–99).
Corrected in all five documents. Paper A's cost bullet is rewritten to lead with "the cost is the snapshot, and it is variable" and quotes the medians with ranges; the essay's cadence paragraph and the index's capture-substrate table now print median-and-range, with an explicit note that the old 7/20/28 and 15–21 s figures were per-duration minima. README's honesty bullet says the same.
R-2  major  The per-snapshot cost decomposition was inverted. The draft implied the ~14–20 s of "missing" cycle time was spread across the budget. In fact pmemsave alone is a median 81% of the cycle (median 7.06 s), with suspend (0.35 s), resume (0.05 s), and the asynchronous read-back off the critical path all negligible. The earlier 0.75 s figure made pmemsave look like the cheap part.
Corrected, and it strengthens the paper's central qualitative claim. "The snapshot primitive sets the cadence, not the 500 ms knob" is now a measurement, not an assertion: the guest idles its requested 0.52 s while the host cycle, dominated by pmemsave, runs 2–24 s. Paper A and the essay now state the cost is pmemsave-dominated and frame the open question as "how to shrink pmemsave," not "how often to sample." The required budget-decomposition figure (round-1 A-2) is now grounded by these medians.
R-3  blocking  Delete-as-you-go (Paper A contribution C1) was not exercised in v3. The 2–3 GiB transient-disk cap is the headline of the streaming-capture contribution, but run_meta.keep_dumps is true on 125 of 128 ok cells. With keep_dumps set, the delete step is disabled: dumps were retained and removed in a single end-of-cell sweep (cell notes show e.g. "removed 29 dumps" after a 28-snapshot cell), so peak transient disk scaled with the trajectory rather than holding to the 2–3 GiB design target.
Retracted to a design target, per author decision. Paper A's abstract and evaluation, the essay, the index table, and README now state that v3 ran with keep_dumps enabled, that the streaming delete-as-you-go cap is an architectural target the campaign did not validate, and that confirming the cap requires a delete-as-you-go re-run. The "B+3.1 streaming helper active" note is kept (the helper did run) but is no longer presented as having bounded transient disk.
R-4  major  The 106× storage reduction is a projection, not a measurement. Re-confirms round-1 A-1 and adds the mechanism: with dumps never retained at campaign scale (and, per R-3, the delete path off), the 5 TiB denominator is a projection of what the 2-D pipeline would have cost, divided into a measured 4.7 MiB trajectory numerator from a differently-sized (90-cell) framing.
Corrected. Every instance of the ratio is now labelled an order-of-magnitude estimate / projection with the 5 TiB called "never fully retained," and the index footnotes the 90-vs-132 campaign-size reconciliation as a pre-submission task.
R-5  major  The leakage-free residual (0.644 vs 0.542) was presented as a genuine signal without a significance test, and it does not survive one. The four replicates per workload-duration are not independent, leaving ≈33 independent groups. A one-sided proportion test gives p≈0.12 at n_eff≈33 (vs. the p≈0.013 one would get by wrongly treating all 118 cells as independent). The gap is not significant at the group level.
Downgraded everywhere. Paper C, the essay, the index KPI, and README now read "at most a weak family hint, statistically indistinguishable from baseline at the replicate-group level," and Paper C's Limitations call for a paired McNemar test and a grouped-bootstrap CI before any "genuine signal" claim. No significance was claimed beyond what the test supports.
R-6  minor  coverage_ratio is not "leakage to audit" — it is the label re-encoded. It takes exactly 0.1333 for every steady cell and 0.200 for every phasic cell, with zero within-family variance (verified in plan03/sweep.csv). Its 1.000 accuracy is definitional, not an empirical leak to be measured.
Sharpened in Paper C and the index: coverage_ratio is described as a constant injective function of the family label, so reproducing the 100% from it alone is tautological. The framing now separates this definitional feature from the two genuinely-leaky family-conditional features (cv_workingset, f1_phase).
R-7  major  The G3 cepstral-SNR gate rests on too few analysis windows for much of the dataset. At window/hop (8,4), 53 of 132 cells yield ≤3 analysis windows and 14 yield none. The defining-metric SNR — the gate that admits the phasic family — is estimated on 1–3 windows for ~40% of cells, where it is fragile.
Documented. Paper B's Limitations gain a bullet stating the DOF problem and recommending G3 be recomputed on a ≥8-window subset (or the SNR gate dropped where DOF is too low); the index and essay both note the SNR "rests on one to three windows" on some cells. This is flagged as a required re-analysis, not silently corrected.
R-8  major  The CUSUM phasic plausibility band is near-unfalsifiable. "5 of 5 phasic workloads pass" rests on the predicted boundary count landing in 1 ≤ n ≤ 20. On trajectories of ≤30 windows a resetting CUSUM lands in that band for almost any non-degenerate input, so the band's acceptance probability under a stationary null is close to 1. The result carries little evidential weight as stated.
Documented in Paper D's Acceptance and Limitations sections: the band is called a weak gate, "5 of 5 pass" is explicitly down-weighted, and the fix — calibrate the band against a permutation / stationary-null distribution and report its false-accept rate — is named as required before this counts as validation.
R-9  minor  The phasic SNR floor was printed as 4.79 dB. The on-disk minimum is 4.7847 dB, which rounds to 4.78.
Corrected to 4.78 in Paper C, the essay, and the index. Caveat: this value was confirmed by the audit agents reading recommendation.json but not independently re-extracted in the final pass (a structural grep came back empty); verify the 4.78 floor against the analyzer output before final submission.

Open items (need a capture re-run or a test, not an edit)

These cannot be fixed by rewording. Each needs the author to run something. They are the round-2 backlog and should be tracked alongside the surviving round-1 items.

  • Delete-as-you-go re-run. Re-capture at least one long cell with keep_dumps=false and record peak transient disk, to validate (or correct) the 2–3 GiB streaming cap that R-3 retracted. This also supplies the measured numerator the storage-ratio claim (R-4) currently lacks.
  • Proper significance test for the family residual. Run a paired McNemar test and a grouped-bootstrap CI on the 0.644-vs-0.542 comparison at the replicate-group level (R-5) before any "genuine signal" language is used.
  • Recompute G3 on adequate DOF. Restrict the cepstral-SNR gate to cells with ≥8 analysis windows, or drop SNR where DOF is too low, then re-report the phasic gate outcomes (R-7).
  • Calibrate the CUSUM plausibility band. Estimate the band's false-accept rate against a permutation / stationary-null distribution; report it next to "5 of 5 phasic pass" (R-8).
  • Leave-one-workload-out CV with CIs. Carried unrun from round-1 B-4: replace leave-one-replicate-out with leave-one-workload-out, seeded repeats, reported confidence intervals.
  • Reconcile campaign size. All storage and compute magnitudes are quoted against a 90-cell framing while the dataset is 132 cells; pick one and restate (round-1 A-1, reaffirmed).
  • refs.bib is mostly placeholders. 8 of 11 entries are TODO stubs needing real citations. Two of them (TODO_ransomware_io_detection, TODO_ransomware_survey) exist only to support the security angle that round-1 B-3 removed — drop or repurpose them.
  • Re-verify the SNR floor. Independently re-extract the 4.78 dB phasic SNR minimum from the analyzer output (R-9); this round confirmed it only via the audit agents' read.

What did not change

The audit did not touch the load-bearing conclusions, because they were already correct or are now better supported:

  • The central thesis holds and is stronger. "A deliberately impoverished single scalar carries only a coarse, weak hint of workload family and nothing finer." R-5 makes the family hint weaker, not stronger, which the thesis already anticipated.
  • The cadence finding is now a measurement. R-2 turns "the snapshot primitive dominates" from an assertion into a measured 81%-of-cycle fact; the 500 ms knob is confirmed inert because the guest idled its requested 0.52 s while the cycle ran an order of magnitude longer.
  • Round-1's leakage finding stands. The 100% classification headline is still an artifact; R-6 only sharpens why (coverage_ratio is the label, not a leak to be measured).
  • Paper D's observability finding stands. Workload markers still do not survive into APF at this cadence; R-8 weakens the plausibility fallback but not the central negative result.
Net. Nine numeric corrections, one retracted contribution (delete-as-you-go → design target), eight open items requiring a re-run. The thesis is unchanged; the numbers now match the disk.

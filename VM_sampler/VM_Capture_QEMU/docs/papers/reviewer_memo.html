Reviewer Memo

Two independent IEEE-style reviews of the Phase-2 v3 results, with the team's point-by-point responses · conducted under the project manager

How to read this

Before the four paper skeletons were frozen, the project manager ran an internal peer review. Two senior reviewers read the data audit, the draft skeletons, and the first-draft synthesis essay independently, with no sight of each other's notes. Each returned a memo; the team answered every point and recorded the resulting file change. This document pairs each critique with its response so the provenance of every revision is auditable.

One review round changed the headline result of the entire portfolio. We have not smoothed that over: the most consequential critique (Reviewer B, B-1) is presented exactly as it landed, including the number it demolished.

Severity legend: blocking must be resolved before submission · major materially changes a claim · minor presentation / positioning.

The review panel

A
Reviewer A — senior academic, systems / virtualization and measurement. Lens: is the capture method sound, is the cost model honest, is it reproducible? Treats superlatives as claims that must be measured, not asserted.
B
Reviewer B — senior academic, machine-learning-for-systems and security. Lens: is the classification valid, is there leakage, does any security framing survive a threat model? Distrusts accuracy figures that look too clean for the information in the signal.

Reviewers are named by role, not person. Their critiques are the substantive ones the data invites; the team's responses are the actual revisions made to the files in this directory.

Reviewer A — systems / measurement

Summary verdict. The capture method (Paper A) is the strongest standalone contribution in the set and is publishable at a measurement or virtualization venue after revisions. The cost claims are real but are currently stated more strongly than they are measured. Tighten the numerator, decompose the cadence budget, and the systems story is solid.

A-1  major  The “106×” storage reduction mixes a measured number with a projection, and two different campaign sizes. The persistent footprint of 4.7 MiB is the measured APF-trajectory size for a 90-cell campaign. The ~5 TiB it is divided against is a projection of what retaining the per-page dumps would have cost — that data was never fully retained — and the v3 dataset is 132 cells, not 90. Dividing a measured 90-cell numerator by a projected mixed-size denominator and leading with a bare “million-fold” superlative is not defensible as written.
Accepted. A reviewer-honesty comment is now baked into Paper A's Evaluation section (paperA_apf_capture/skeleton.tex): reconcile to a single campaign size, report a measured numerator or explicitly label the 5 TiB as a projection, and present the ratio as an order-of-magnitude estimate rather than a headline. The portfolio index now footnotes the storage magnitudes as quoted against the 90-cell framing and flags the 132-vs-90 reconciliation as a pre-submission task.
A-2  major  The cadence-floor finding is the paper's best result but is asserted, not broken down. “15–21 s/snap, pause fraction 0.96–0.97, pmemsave ≈ 0.75 s” is compelling, but a reader cannot see where the other ~14–20 s goes. Give the per-duration snapshot counts as a table and decompose the per-snapshot budget into suspend, pmemsave write, resume, and asynchronous helper read-back. Otherwise “the snapshot primitive dominates” is a claim, not a measurement.
Accepted. The per-duration counts are grounded (7 / 20 / 28 snapshots at 120 / 300 / 600 s) and now appear as an explicit results table stub in Paper A; a budget-decomposition figure (suspend / pmemsave / resume / helper read-back) is listed as a required figure. The cadence floor remains the load-bearing systems result and motivates Paper B's single-interval design.
A-3  minor  State plainly that this is offline research capture. A 96% guest-pause fraction means the method as implemented is not a production-latency introspector. Don't let a reader assume otherwise.
Already present in Paper A's Limitations; we strengthened the wording and named live dirty-bit tracking as the natural production optimization.
A-4  minor  Position against the right related work. The novelty of APF is that it is content-derived (a byte difference) and capture-external, versus hypervisor dirty-bit / working-set estimators and live-migration dirty-page tracking, which use hardware bits. Cite those explicitly; they are the honest baseline, not generic VMI.
Accepted. refs.bib carries placeholders for working-set estimation / dirty-page tracking and KSM / memory deduplication, and Paper A's Background section frames APF against them. These citations are marked TODO-verify and must be filled before submission.

Reviewer B — ML-for-systems / security

Summary verdict. Paper C as first drafted — “100% phasic-vs-steady, a memory-signal ransomware-rhythm detector” — is a reject. The number is an artifact and the security framing is unsupported. Recast as a leakage-controlled measurement of how little workload identity survives a coarse scalar, it becomes a genuinely useful, cautionary contribution. Paper D's observability finding is correct and should be published as such, but it must not claim marker-validated segmentation.

B-1  blocking  100% accuracy from a single coarse scalar is not plausible. Audit for feature leakage before you publish it. A RandomForest reporting a [[54,0],[0,64]] confusion matrix on a one-number-per-snapshot signal is a red flag, not a triumph. My prior: one of your ten features is computed conditionally on the family label the analyzer already assigned — a per-family constant, or complementary not-a-number missingness. Ablate every family-conditional feature and re-report against a majority baseline with a confidence interval. I expect the “perfect” separation to collapse.
Confirmed, in full. The engineering group wrote paperC_family_classification/leakage_ablation.py and re-ran the binary task under the same leave-one-replicate-out protocol. Three of the ten features are family-conditional: cv_workingset (computed only for steady cells, NaN for every phasic cell), f1_phase (computed only for phasic cells, NaN for every steady cell — complementary missingness that encodes the label), and coverage_ratio (assigned a per-family constant, 0.133 to steady and 0.200 to phasic). The headline is retracted. Paper C is recast (see B-3); the synthesis essay now leads with the trade “the 100% is feature construction, not behavior,” and this is recorded as the fourth honest finding of the project.
B-2  major  The stated mechanism for separation is also wrong. The draft explained the 100% by “phasic cepstral SNR high (4.7–6.4 dB), steady SNR low.” That is false on your own data: steady SNR spans 2.65–7.99 dB and overlaps the phasic range; the steady hash-table workload has the highest SNR in the whole set. Cepstral SNR does not separate the families — it cannot be the mechanism.
Accepted and verified. Cepstral SNR alone classifies at 0.508 (chance). The “phasic-high / steady-low SNR” mechanism is explicitly retracted in Paper C's Results, and the essay states that steady SNR overlaps phasic SNR with the hash-table workload on top.
B-3  blocking  There is no security claim here, and the paper must stop implying one. The “phasic” class is synthetic reversible-XOR sandboxes that emulate an encryption write-rhythm; the “steady” class is memory micro-benchmarks. There is no benign-but-bursty control — exactly where false positives would live — no threat model, no base rate, no open-set evaluation. Calling this a ransomware detector is not supportable.
Accepted. Paper C is retitled “How Much Workload Identity Survives in a Single-Scalar Memory-Liveness Trajectory? A Leakage-Controlled Study,” retargeted to an ML-for-systems / measurement venue, and its security claim removed. A Limitations & Security section now lists what would be required before any security framing: an explicit threat model and base rate, a real-malware corpus, a benign-phasic control class, open-set / unknown-workload evaluation, and ROC at a deployment-realistic base rate rather than balanced accuracy.
B-4  major  Your cross-validation grouping is too weak. Leave-one-replicate-out keeps four replicates of the same workload-duration in play and does not break duration / capture-geometry structure. Prefer leave-one-workload-out, and report confidence intervals from repeated, seeded grouped CV — a single point accuracy on 118 cells is not enough.
Accepted into Paper C's Limitations as a required methodology change for the full paper. The leakage ablation is the priority correction; leave-one-workload-out with seeded repeats and reported CIs is the next experiment, not yet run.
B-5  minor  “100%” needs an interval even when it is real. 118/118 has a Clopper–Pearson 95% lower bound of 0.969 — “perfect” is never the honest single number.
Added; the bound is stated in Paper C's Results, alongside the note that on this task it is not the relevant number anyway, because the 100% is leakage.
B-6  minor  Paper D: do not claim you segment phases correctly. Validating phasic segmentation by boundary-count plausibility is acceptable as a fallback, but it is not ground truth. Lead with the observability finding — the markers do not survive into APF — and label the strong claim as deferred.
Already the team's stance and unchanged: Paper D's contribution is the robust family-aware detector plus the documented finding that workload markers are not APF observables (zero marker-rich cells; compounded by a capture-side wall-clock skew). The strong claim — “we segment phases correctly” — is explicitly deferred to a capture that carries markers into the recorded signal.

Joint recommendation

The reviewers converged on a consolidation that the team adopts as the working venue plan. The result map (four results) need not be the venue map (four papers):

Systems paper — A + B merged

The capture method and cost model (A) and the acceptance-gated tuning protocol (B) tell one systems story: a cheap single-scalar liveness signal, a measured cost floor set by the snapshot primitive, and a gate framework that certifies “present” vs. “adequately sampled.” Target: measurement / virtualization venue.

Characterization paper — C + D merged

The leakage-controlled family/instance study (C) and the family-aware segmenter (D) tell one workload-characterization story, with D as the “what next.” Central message: a coarse scalar carries a weak family hint and no instance identity, and even the family hint must be measured past feature-construction leakage. Target: ML-for-systems / measurement venue. Explicitly not a security-detection venue on this evidence.

PM decision. Keep all four skeletons as the analytical decomposition (they isolate the four results cleanly), but write toward two submissions. Revisit the mapping once a target venue and author list are fixed.
